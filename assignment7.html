Gabrielle's Final Proejct

Concept: Body poetry

In my other life I was a literature student and I discovered poetry when I was
17 or 18, with T.S. Eliot. The love was instant. I was still learning English at the
time and was mesmerized by the rhythms, the sounds and the images this
language could produced.

I've been playing with text generating neural nets for the past 6 months or so
and want to create a piece that incorporates some of that work with the tactility
and physicality of a 3D printed piece. The idea is to create a wearable
that features a small ML system running on a tiny CMU equipped with accelerometers
that would map gestures to words, so that you could compose poems
by moving.

There are a bunch of boards out there that fit the bill. The Sparkfun Edge
Board is one (on the left). Arduino also makes one, and so does Ada fruit, which is the one I have. (right)

My plan is to design a bracelet in Rhino and print it. It'll have to be sturdy
enough for dancing and accomodate the ports, lights en LCD screen of my Ada board.

Plan

1. Take my board measurements.

2. Design my bracelet in Rhino.

3. Export STL, slice and print.

4. Test and adjust.
